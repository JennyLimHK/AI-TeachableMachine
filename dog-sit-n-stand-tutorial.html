AI Dog: Sit & Stand!

A Scratch Tutorial using Artificial Intelligence

⭐ Ages 7–12  |  Beginner–Intermediate
What will you build?
In this project, you'll make a smart virtual dog that listens to your command. When you say sit, the dog sits! When you say stand, the dog stands! It uses real Artificial Intelligence (AI) to recognise your movements. How cool is that? 🎉

🤖 How Does the AI Work?

This project uses a special tool called Teachable Machine (made by Google). Someone already taught the computer what "sit" and "stand" sounds like — by giving it lots of examples using a microphone, just like teaching a real dog!

When you run the project, Scratch loads this AI model and uses your microphone to check what you might be saying at every moment. It then tells Scratch: "The person said sit!" or "The person said stand!" — and the dog reacts instantly.

AI learns from examples, just like you learn from practice. The more examples it sees, the smarter it gets!

🔍 Understanding the Code

Let's look at the blocks that make the magic happen. Click on the dog sprite (the picture of the dog) and look at the code in the middle of the screen.

You will see three groups of blocks:

A

Setting Up (When the Green Flag is Clicked)

When clicked
show
use AI model [Teachable Machine URL]
start microphone sampling
switch costume to [standing-dog]

This group runs as soon as you press the green flag. Here's what each block does:

  • show — makes the dog visible on screen.
  • use AI model — loads the trained AI from Teachable Machines URL so Scratch knows what "sit" and "stand" sound like.
  • start audio sampling — turns on your microphone and starts listening to tiny slices of sound — thousands of them every second. These slices are called samples. It's like taking lots of super-fast snapshots, but for sound instead of pictures.
  • switch costume to standing-dog — the dog starts in a standing position.
B

When the AI Hears "Sit"

When AI hears [Sit]
switch costume to [sitting-dog]

Whenever the AI model decides that you've commanded the dog to sit, Scratch changes the dog's costume to the sitting dog picture. The dog sits down — just like you ordered it to!

C

When the AI Hears "Stand"

When AI detects [Stand]
switch costume to [standing-dog]

Whenever the AI model decides that you've commanded the dog to stand, Scratch switches the display to the standing dog costume. The dog stands up again!

The dog has two costumes — one for sitting and one for standing. Switching costumes is how Scratch animates the characters (known as sprites) shown onstage!

▶️ Let's Run It!

1

Press the Green Flag

Click the big green flag button at the top of the screen. The dog will appear, and the camera will turn on.

2

Stand Up!

Make sure your microphone is turned on and the room is relatively quiet. Say "stand" clearly into the mic. The dog should be standing too!

3

Sit Down!

Now say "sit". Watch the dog — it should sit down too!

4

Try Again and Again

Say "stand", "sit", "stand", "sit", "sit", "stand", and so on. The dog follows you every time (or at least most of the time, depending on how accurately the A.I. model makes decisions). You are controlling a virtual dog with your voice! 🐾

If the dog doesn't react, check that your mic is on and your voice can be heard clearly. Make sure you are in a relatively quiet room.

🧩 Key Scratch Concepts You Learned

ConceptWhat It Means
🟠 EventsBlocks that start when something happens, like clicking the flag or the AI detecting a pose
🟣 CostumesDifferent images for a sprite — switching them creates animation
🟢 AI ExtensionExtra blocks that let Scratch connect to AI tools like Teachable Machine
📷 Audio SamplingUsing the microphone to gather audio input over and over, very quickly. The AI looks at these samples and asks: "Does this sound like one of the words I learned?" If enough samples in a row sound like "stand", the AI decides that it heard the word "stand" and it moves the dog according to your command. That's why you sometimes need to speak clearly and wait a moment — the AI is collecting enough samples to be really sure it heard you correctly before it acts!

💬 Let's Talk About AI

The AI in this project was trained by feeding it many audio clips of people saying "sit" and "stand". The more examples it hears, the better it gets at telling them apart. This type of AI is called audio classification — it classifies (sorts) sounds into corresponding words.

You just used voice recognition AI — the same technology that powers Siri, Google Assistant, and Amazon Alexa! These systems are trained on millions of voice recordings so they can understand many different accents, speeds, and tones of voice.
